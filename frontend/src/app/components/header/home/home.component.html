ABOUT THE DEPARTMENT

The Department of Mining Engineering at Indian Institute of Technology was started in 1956, with the approval of the Planning Commission and the All-India Council of Technical Education (AICTE). The first batch of twenty B.Tech students graduated in 1961 and currently the sanctioned strength of the undergraduate program is 95.

Vision

To be a center of excellence in Mining sciences and engineering education and research producing global leaders in engineering, technology and management.

MISSION

  • To provide up-to-date education and training to students in mining and earth sciences, oil and natural gas, and mineral beneficiation.
  • To develop the best expertise in mining and allied technology and management to impart overall training to students in visualizing, synthesizing and executing projects.
  • To incubate a spirit of entrepreneurship and innovation.
  • To undertake sponsored research and provide consultancy services in industrial, educational and society-relevant areas.

Message from the Head

Raw materials demand for the welfare and subsistence needs of mankind is ever increasing with the advancement of science and technology. However, the mineral market often faces challenges due to the uniqueness of mineral resources. This puts mining and mineral resource engineers under pressure to find new ways of production, beneficiation, and marketing of coal, different ores, and petroleum. As an important stakeholder in the mineral industry, the mining academics at IIT Kharagpur have been trying their best to produce quality manpower to serve the mining and allied industries. The skills learned here enable graduates to excel in diverse fields, including finance and digital sectors. We expect that, in the future, the Indian mining industry will retain some of our best graduates to serve the country's mineral exploitation needs.
