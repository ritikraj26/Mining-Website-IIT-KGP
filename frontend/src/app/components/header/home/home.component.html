ABOUT THE DEPARTMENT

The Department of Mining Engineering at Indian Institute of Technology was started in 1956, with the approval of the Planning Commission and the All-India Council of Technical Education (AICTE). The first batch of twenty B.Tech students graduated in 1961 and currently the sanctioned strength of the undergraduate program is 95.

Vision

To be a center of excellence in Mining sciences and engineering education and research producing global leaders in engineering,technology and management.

MISSION

  • To provide up-to-date education and training to the students in mining and earth sciences, oil and natural gas and mineral beneficiation.
  • To develop the best expertise in mining and allied technology and management to impart overall training to students in visualizing, synthesizing and executing projects.
  • To incubate a spirit of entrepreneurship and innovation
  • To undertake sponsored research and provide consultancy services in industrial, educational and society relevant areas

Message from the Head

Raw materials demand for the welfare and subsistence needs of mankind is ever increasing with the advancement of science and technology. However, the mineral market often faces challenges due to uniqueness of mineral resources. This puts the mining and mineral resource engineers under challenge to find new ways of production, beneficiation and marketing of coal, different ores and petroleum. To this end as an important stakeholder of the mineral industry, the mining academics at IIT Kharagpur have been trying their best to produce quality manpower to serve the mining and allied industries. The skills learned herein enable the graduates to develop opportunities to play their in wide ranging fields, including in the finance and digital sectors.
